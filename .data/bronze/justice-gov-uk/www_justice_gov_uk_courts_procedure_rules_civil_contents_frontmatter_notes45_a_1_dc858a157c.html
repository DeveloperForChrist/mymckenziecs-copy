Notes to Accompany 1st October 2007 45th Update

Coming into force

All the amendments included in this update come into force on 01 October 2007 with the following exceptions:

1st July 2007

Part 6, Part 12 and Part 74 (where amended by the Civil Jurisdiction and Judgments Regulations 2007)

Introduction

The 45th Update to the Civil Procedure Rules introduces changes in a large number of areas:

Part 6, Part 12 and Part 74 are amended by the Civil Jurisdiction and Judgments Regulations 2007.

PD 7 and PD 74 are amended to incorporate the changes into the practice directions.

PD 8, Part 52 and PD 52 are amended to include a number of claims, applications and appeals previously dealt with in scheduled rules. A number of scheduled rules have been revoked as a consequence.

Part 19 is amended and a new PD 19C is inserted to incorporate a new two-stage procedure for permission to proceed in derivative actions as a consequence of the Companies Act 2006.

Part 21 and PD21 are amended to reflect new concepts created by the Mental Capacity Act 2005, in particular the new test for capacity (which is decision and time specific) is reflected in the procedures set out for civil courts.

Part 2, PD 4, Part 6, PD 7C, PD 7E, PD 8, PD 10, Part 12, PD 12, Part 14, PD 22, PD 23, PD 23B, PD 25B, PD26B, Part 30, Part 32, PD 37, Part 39, PD39, PD40B, PD41, PD41B, Part 45, Part 46, Part 47, Part 48, PD 43 to 48 and PD 52 are also amended to incorporate the changes made to Part 21.

PD 21 is also amended with to improve procedure relating to the investment of children’s funds.

PD 37 is amended to simplify the procedure for notifying litigants that funds are available should an offeree wish to accept a Part 36 offer.

PD39 is amended to provide that an appeal hearing against the decision of a Registration Officer, who has refused an application for anonymous registration on the electoral register, should be heard in private.

Part 46 is amended to increase the amount of costs which the court may award as the costs of an advocate for preparing for and appearing at the trial of a claim in the fast track for trials commencing on, or after 1st October 2007.

Part 47 is amended to increase the time within which an appeal decision of an authorised court officer relating to detailed assessment of costs may be filed from 14 to 21 days.

Part 49, PD 49 and PD49B are amended to incorporate provisions in the Companies Act 2006 coming into force on 1st October 2007.

Part 52 is amended to introduce a standard procedure for third parties to apply to intervene in statutory appeals.

PD 54 is amended to include a centralised address for service of a claim form for judicial review where the defendant or interested party is the Asylum & Immigration Tribunal.

PD 55 and PD 55B are amended to allow claimants to input multiple accounts onto PCOL by entering a global total for arrears on all accounts; and to ensure that claimants are informed of where they should be including details of statutory grounds on possession claims. Please note the temporary nature of some of these amendments.

PD 57, PD 59, PD 60, PD63, PD 73, the Directors Disqualification Proceedings PD and the Insolvency Proceedings PD are amended to show the transfer of Cheshire to the North West Region of HM Courts Service, and the Technology and Construction Court move from Salford county court to Manchester Civil Justice Centre.

Part 65, PD 2B and PD 65 are amended to include provisions on parenting contracts and parenting orders in the Anti-social Behaviour Act 2003 allowing local authorities and registered social landlords to enter into parenting contracts and apply for parenting orders as a consequence of Section 24 of the Police and Justice Act 2006. The provisions relating to anti-social behaviour injunctions are also amended as a consequence of Section 26 & 27 of the Police and Justice Act 2006.

The Insolvency Proceedings PD is amended to require an appellant in insolvency proceedings to provide both a transcript of judgement and skeleton argument at the permission stage of an appeal; and to include a sub-paragraph omitted in error in the 2000 update.

The Pre-Action Protocol for Housing Disrepair Cases has been amended to correct an error that occurred when the provisions relating to Alternative Dispute Resolution were incorporated.

A full list of the changes is set out below.
