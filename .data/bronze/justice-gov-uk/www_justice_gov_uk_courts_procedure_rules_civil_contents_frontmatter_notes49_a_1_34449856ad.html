Notes to Accompany 6th April 2009 49th Update

Coming into force

All the amendments including in this update come into force on 6 April 2009 with the following exceptions:

Part 79 in relation to Financial Restrictions proceedings under the Counter-Terrorism Act 2008 came into force 4 December 2008; and

Practice Direction 52 Appeals. Changes in relation to statutory appeals come into force on 9 January 2008.

Introduction

The 49th Update to the Civil Procedure Rules introduces changes in a large number of areas:

Consequential amendments have been made to Part 1, Part 3, Part 7, Part 14, Part 16, Part 30, and RSC O.115 and to PD55, PD64, the PD on Devolution Issues, the Pre Action Protocol for Judicial review and to the Glossary.

Part 7 How to start proceedings – the Claim Form and PD7C Production Centre

Amendments allow for claims to be issued electronically through the Money Claim Online and Court Production Centre where the particulars of claim are to be served separately.

PD7D Claims for the recovery of taxes

Amendments extend the list of taxes and duties subject to the streamlined procedure set out in PD7D. These include student loan repayments deductible by or recoverable from the employer, VAT, insurance Premium Stamp Duty Land Tax, Environmental Taxes, and Duties of Custom and Excise.

Part 14 Admissions

An amendment to Rule 14.1A(2)(a) as a consequence of a new PD on Pre Action Conduct.

Part 26 Case Management

For those claims issued on or after 6 April 2009 the financial limit of the fast track procedure is increased from £15,000 to £25,000.

Part 44 General Rules about Costs and PD supplements Parts 43–48 Costs

New rules to allow applications for and variation of costs capping orders on future costs are inserted and the Practice Direction is amended.

Part 46 Fast Track Trial Costs

The table setting out the amount of fast track trial costs, which may be awarded by the court, is amended. An additional entry of £1,650 is inserted for those fast track cases where the value of the claim is more than £15,000. The original entry is modified to cover claims in the £10,000 – £15,000 band.

PD52 Appeals

As a consequence of the creation of a new Tribunal systems, including the new Upper Tribunal PD52 is amended. These changes come into force on 9 January 2009.

PD54D Judicial Review (Administrative Court (Venue)

A new Practice Direction supplementing Part 54 is inserted setting out the procedures for issue, administration and hearing of cases outside London and Cardiff.

Part 55 Possession Claims and PD55

This Part is amended to extend the period of notice about a possession claim that must be given by the claimant to the occupiers of the relevant property.

Part 70 General Rules about Enforcement of Judgments and Orders and PD70

This provision enables the enforcement of negotiated settlements in employment tribunal cases to be enforced as if payable under a court order.

Part 75 Traffic Enforcement and PD75

This amendment enables requests for a review of a court officer’s decision to be determined without an oral hearing unless the person making their request asks for a hearing or the court orders a hearing. Provision is also made to enable an authority to request reissue of warrants (within the 12 month validity period) where the respondent’s address has changed since the issue of the warrant.

Part 79 Financial Restrictions proceedings under the Counter – Terrorism Act 2008

This new Part came into force on 4 December 2008 and provides for financial restrictions proceedings, including the application to the High Court to set aside a financial restrictions decision.

CCR Order 26 Warrants of Execution, Delivery and Possession

This amendment requires a person requesting a warrant of possession to be issued to file a request certifying that the land, which is subject of the judgement or order, has not been vacated.

Practice Direction – Protocols

A new Practice Direction is created on the Pre-Action Conduct of parties. This includes the requirement in business debt claims for the creditor to provide information about sources of advice to the debtor before issuing proceedings. A consequential amendment is made to Rule 14.1A92)(a) on introduction of this new Practice Direction.
