Notes to Accompany 6th April 2008 46th Update

Coming into force

The amendments included in this update come into force on 06 April 2008 with the following exceptions:

14th January 2008

  • Amendments to PD 49 relating to the
    Companies (Cross-Border Mergers) Regulations 2007; and
  • PD 52, paragraph 2A.1

31st March 2008

  • Amendments to Part 75 and the Accompanying
    Practice Direction.

01st April 2008

  • Amendments to the Civil Recovery
    Proceedings Practice Direction; and
  • PD 47, paragraph 49A.5(3).

Introduction

The 46th Update to the Civil Procedure Rules introduces changes in a large number of areas:

Part 3 is amended to extend the sanction of striking out a claim to include the non-payment of a hearing fee or court fee (where the claim proceeds on the counterclaim alone) and replace reference to ‘exemption or remission’ of court fees with ‘full or part remission’ of court fees to reflect amendments to the Civil Proceedings Fees Order 2004 (S.I. 2004/3121).

Part 19 is amended to correct a couple of minor inconsistencies occurring after procedure consequential on the Companies Act 2006 was implemented in the 45th update.

Part 52 is amended to correct a typographical error appearing in the heading to one of the Sections.

Part 54 is amended to reflect an amendment to the Supreme Court Act 1981 that allows the High Court to substitute its own decision for that of inferior courts and tribunals in judicial review proceedings. A number of rules in this Part relating to statutory appeals under section 101 of the Nationality Immigration and Asylum Act 2002 have been revoked as this section of the Act has now been repealed.

Part 57 is amended to bring it in line with the new concepts created by the Mental Capacity Act and changes to the rules in the 45th update.

Part 75 and the accompanying practice direction is amended as a consequence of regulations relating to parking enforcement made by the Department for Transport and the Welsh Assembly Government under section 82 of the Traffic Management Act 2004 (Traffic Enforcement provisions).

Part 76 is amended to deal with drafting comments made by the Joint Committee on Statutory Instruments on the rules governing control order court procedures.

A new part 77 introduces procedures to enable applications for, or relating to serious crime prevention orders introduced by the Serious Crime Act 2007. A new practice direction supports the new rules dealing with these applications.

RSC Order 96 is revoked. This scheduled rule has been incorporated into part 8 in the 45th update to allow for modernisation.

PD3D is created to deal with court procedure for mesothelioma claims.

PD8 is amended to include reference to proceedings under the Trustee Act 1925 in the table specifying the Division to which an application is assigned for applications in the High Court.

PD16 is amended to correct a typographical error where the wrong paragraph is referenced in the text.

PD19C is amended to ensure consistency with the rules governing procedure for derivative claims.

PD47 and the Civil Recovery Proceedings PD is amended as a result of the abolition of the Asset Recovery Agency by the Serious Crime Act 2007.

PD 49 is amended to incorporate provisions of the Companies Act 2006 coming into force in April 2008, and to provide a new High Court procedure to deal with the cross border merger of limited liability companies under the Companies (Cross-Border Mergers) Regulations 2007.

PD52 is amended to enable third parties to make representations in applications for, or relating to serious crime prevention orders and table 1 has been amended to clarify the appeal routes for final decisions in Part 7 and Part 8 claims.

PD55B has been amended to remove the temporary provision inserted in the 45th update to ensure that claimants were informed of where they should be including details of statutory grounds on possession claims.

PD69 has been amended to include reference to the Insolvency Practitioners Regulations 2005 which replace the similarly titled 1990 regulations.

The PD Governing Proceedings under Enactments Relating to Discrimination is amended as a consequence of the Equality Act 2006.

The PD (Bills of Sale), supplementing RSC Order 95 is revoked as the relevant contents of RSC Order 95 were incorporated into practice direction 8 in the 45th update.
